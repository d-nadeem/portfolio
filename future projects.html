3D Octopus Arm End Effector

Problem Statement

Vacuum end effectors struggle to grip irregularly shaped items because they rely on flat, cubic surfaces. An octopus has the ability to conform its arms to an object's shape. Mimicking the ability enables an end effector to grip an irregular object. This project builds upon a research paper, by improving on the design for assembly, reducing cost, and enable maintenance.

Contribution

What?

Redesigned CAD model based on the research paper “https://www.cell.com/device/pdfExtended/S2666-9986(24)00603-3.” Improved the design by developing components for manufacturing, assembly, and maintenance.

How?

Removed non-essential features from the CAD model, iterated the design through testing the CAD assembly, and 3D-printed models.
